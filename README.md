# Financial Complaints Dashboard

## 1. Project Overview

This Tableau dashboard analyzes financial consumer complaints submitted between December 2011 and October 2020. It visualizes complaint patterns across issues, states, products, and communication media, providing a data-driven overview of consumer experiences and financial institutions’ responsiveness.

The dataset offers valuable insights into:

- Volume and types of complaints
- Response effectiveness and dispute resolution rates
- Geographic distribution of consumer dissatisfaction
- Communication channels used by consumers

The goal is to help stakeholders identify trends, root causes, and opportunities for process improvement in customer complaint management.

## 2. Executive Summary

Between 2011 and 2020, a total of 75,513 financial complaints were reported.

Key highlights include:

- **High Timely Response Rate:** 98.1% of complaints were addressed on time — indicating strong operational responsiveness.
- **Dispute Rate:** 9.8% of cases were disputed by consumers, showing a moderate dissatisfaction rate.
- **Resolved at No Cost:** 15.5% of complaints were resolved at no cost to consumers, suggesting fair resolution practices.
- **Top Complaint Categories:** Most complaints were related to account management, deposits and withdrawals, and payment processing issues.
- **Top Complaint States:** California (CA), Texas (TX), Florida (FL), and New York (NY) recorded the highest complaint volumes.
- **Primary Reporting Channel:** 50.4% of complaints were filed via the web, followed by referrals (29.5%) and phone (11.6%).

## 3. Insights Deep Dive

### A. Complaint Volume and Responsiveness

- **Total Complaints:** 75,513
- **Timely Response:** 98.1% – consistently high across years, showing operational maturity and adherence to SLAs.
- **In Progress:** Only 283 cases (0.37%) remain unresolved, indicating efficiency in closing issues.
- **Historical Trend:** The complaint volume fluctuated but maintained an upward trend post-2015, reflecting growing digital banking adoption and consumer awareness.

**Story:**  
As online banking and fintech services grew during the 2015–2020 period, complaint volume spiked — particularly around payment and account management issues. However, institutions adapted quickly, maintaining nearly 98% timely response rate despite the surge.

### B. Complaints by Issue

- **Managing an Account:** Highest category, indicating users face frequent access, login, or service-related problems.
- **Deposits and Withdrawals** and **Payment Process Issues** also form large portions, showing friction in transactional systems.
- **Struggling to Pay Mortgage** and **Billing Disputes** remain steady contributors, linked to loan management and billing transparency.

**Quantified Insight:**  
Together, the top three categories contribute to over 60% of total complaints, signaling key focus areas for financial process improvement.

### C. Complaints by Product

- **Credit Card:** 25.4%
- **Checking/Savings Accounts:** 17.8%
- **Mortgage:** 16.5%
- **Debt Collection:** 9.5%

**Trend Observation:**  
Credit card–related issues consistently dominate, possibly due to billing errors and payment disputes. Mortgages and savings account complaints indicate ongoing struggles with repayment and fund access processes.

### D. Complaints by Media

- **Web Submissions:** 50.4% — most popular and fastest channel.
- **Referrals:** 29.5% — possibly from affiliated organizations or legal routes.
- **Phone & Postal Mail:** 18.6% combined — show traditional channels still used by some demographics.

**Story:**  
The web channel’s dominance highlights the digital maturity of consumers, aligning with increased fintech engagement post-2015.

### E. Complaints by State

**Top 4 States:** California (CA), Texas (TX), Florida (FL), and New York (NY).  
These states represent high population density and major financial hubs — naturally correlating with higher complaint volumes.

**Quantified Insight:**  
These four states collectively account for over 40% of total complaints — a critical concentration area for regional process audits.

### F. Consumer Dispute Rate

- **Yes (Disputed):** 9.8%
- **No:** 41.3%
- **N/A:** 48.9%

**Interpretation:**  
While the majority of consumers accept resolutions, nearly 1 in 10 disputes the company’s response, signaling potential dissatisfaction in communication clarity or policy transparency.
